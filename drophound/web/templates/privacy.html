Privacy Policy

How we handle your data.

Last updated: June 21, 2026 · Effective date: June 21, 2026

DropHound ("we", "us", "our") operates the website at drophound.app (the "Service"). This policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, and your rights over it. We keep it plain — no legalese traps.

1. Data We Collect

  • Email address — provided when you register, subscribe to alerts, or contact us.
  • Password — stored as a one-way hash (scrypt). We cannot read your password.
  • Telegram handle — optional, only if you choose to receive Telegram alerts.
  • Watchlist & collection data — the products you choose to watch or add to your collection tracker.
  • Session cookie — a random ID (cookie name dh_sid) stored in your browser to keep you logged in. It contains no personal information.
  • Log data — standard server logs: IP address, browser type, pages visited, timestamps. Retained for up to 30 days for security and debugging.
  • Payment data — we do not store card numbers or full payment details. Payments are processed by Stripe, Inc. We only store your email, Stripe customer ID, and subscription status.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • To deliver restock alerts and digest emails you signed up for.
  • To maintain your account, watchlist, and premium subscription.
  • To detect and prevent fraud, bot activity, and abuse.
  • To improve the Service through aggregated, anonymised usage analytics.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not use your data for targeted advertising. We do not build profiles on you for marketing purposes.

3. Cookies

We use one essential cookie: dh_sid, a session identifier that keeps you logged in. It is set as HttpOnly and SameSite=Lax, and is transmitted only over HTTPS in production. It expires after one year of inactivity.

We do not use advertising cookies, third-party tracking cookies, or analytics cookies that share data with external parties. A localStorage key (dh_cookie_ok) records your acknowledgement of this banner — it stays in your browser only and is never sent to us.

4. Third-Party Services

  • Stripe, Inc. — payment processing. Card data never touches our servers. See Stripe's privacy policy.
  • Resend / email delivery — transactional emails (alerts, receipts). Your email address is shared with Resend solely to deliver those emails.
  • Telegram / Discord — if you opt in to bot alerts, your Telegram handle or membership in a Discord server is used to deliver notifications. We do not control those platforms.
  • Google Firebase / Firestore — we may store a security event audit trail (login timestamps, IP addresses, account changes) in Google Cloud Firestore for fraud detection and account recovery. Data is stored in the United States. See Firebase privacy policy.
  • Affiliate links — when you click Buy or Resale links, you are redirected through affiliate programmes (eBay Partner Network, Amazon Associates, StockX). Those platforms set their own cookies and track their own conversions. We do not share your personal data with them beyond the referral click.
  • Google Fonts — font assets are loaded from Google's CDN. Google may log your IP address.

5. Data Retention

  • Account data is retained until you delete your account.
  • Security event logs are retained for up to 12 months.
  • Server access logs are retained for up to 30 days.
  • If you cancel your premium subscription, we retain your email and account history to allow reactivation; we do not delete it automatically.

6. Your Rights (GDPR — EEA / UK users)

If you are in the European Economic Area or UK, you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate data.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — delete your account and associated data. You can do this from your account settings, or email us.
  • Restriction — ask us to pause processing your data in certain circumstances.
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Object — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Lodge a complaint — with your local data protection authority.

The legal basis for processing is: contract performance (delivering the Service you signed up for) and legitimate interests (fraud prevention, security logging). Where we rely on consent (optional Telegram / Discord alerts), you can withdraw it at any time.

7. Your Rights (CCPA — California users)

California residents have the right to know what personal information we collect and how it is used, the right to delete personal information, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising these rights. We do not sell personal information as defined under the CCPA.

To exercise any right, email us at the address below. We will respond within 45 days.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under 13 (or 16 in the EEA). We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us personal data, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

9. Security

We use industry-standard measures: TLS encryption in transit, scrypt password hashing, session tokens stored as HttpOnly cookies, CSRF protection on all forms, rate limiting, and a security audit trail in Firebase. No system is perfectly secure — if you discover a vulnerability, please disclose it responsibly by emailing us.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy. Material changes will be announced via email or a prominent notice on the site. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of the Service after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
